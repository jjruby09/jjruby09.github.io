Work

Background & Experience

Three domains, one methodology — Bayesian inference and data-driven modelling applied to complex physical systems.

Physics

High-Energy-Density Physics & Fusion Science

Laboratory for Laser Energetics · Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory · University of Rochester

My scientific foundation was built studying inertial confinement fusion (ICF) and high-energy-density (HED) physics — regimes where intense laser pulses drive matter to extreme pressures and temperatures. My Ph.D. thesis, Bayesian Inference of Fundamental Physics at Extreme Conditions, developed new statistical frameworks for extracting physical information from these experiments.

Experimental Design

Proposed, designed, simulated, and conducted experiments on the OMEGA laser system studying laser-driven implosion dynamics, converging shock waves, energy flow in thin shell implosions, and nuclear burn physics.

Bayesian Diagnostics

Applied Bayesian inference and MCMC methods to diagnose implosion performance from nuclear and X-ray observables, constraining physical models at gigabar pressures and separating signal from complex experimental noise.

Simulation & Modelling

Built simulation workflows coupling radiation-hydrodynamics codes to diagnostic forward models. Developed novel thermal physics models and analytic solutions for converging shock problems.

National Ignition Facility

Built analysis tools for x-ray imaging at the NIF. Contributed to understanding of stagnation physics and residual velocity measurements in ICF implosion experiments.

Baseball

Baseball Research & Development

Houston Astros · Senior Director, R&D (2023–Present) · Lead Innovator (2022–2023)

Leading the R&D function for one of Major League Baseball's most analytically sophisticated organizations. The methodology transfers directly from physics: build honest probabilistic models of complex systems, test them against observed outcomes, and inform decisions under uncertainty.

Research Leadership

Oversee ~20 technical staff including post-graduate researchers and software engineers. Set the technical and research direction for the organization and manage ~$5M annual budget.

Bayesian-Physical Modelling

Apply Bayesian-physical models to baseball systems including biomechanics, rigid body mechanics, and aerodynamics. Build probabilistic models of player performance and development trajectories.

Software Infrastructure

Direct the architectural design and development of the Astros' research and product software infrastructure. Manage relationships and contracts with external research partners and vendors.

Cross-Organizational Impact

Collaborate across the organization to drive progress in all areas of baseball operations — translating statistical findings into actionable, decision-relevant outputs for scouts, coaches, and front-office leadership.
