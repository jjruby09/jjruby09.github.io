About

The Core Thread

Analysis of physical systems — from laser-driven fusion implosions to the mechanics of professional baseball — using Bayesian statistical methods and modern data analysis to extract physical insights from complex data.

I am a researcher broadly interested in the analysis of physical systems. My work focuses on high-energy-density physics with an emphasis on Bayesian statistical methods, and I am also interested in the physics and modelling of professional baseball. I am particularly interested in using modern data analysis techniques to extract physical insights from complex data sets.

My career has been structured around one question: what can the data actually tell us? The methodology is the same whether you're diagnosing a laser-driven fusion implosion or projecting a pitcher's future performance — build rigorous probabilistic models, test them against expensive data, and make decisions under meaningful uncertainty.
