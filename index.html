At the Intersection of Science & Sport

My career has been structured around one question: what can the data actually tell us? Not what we want it to say, not what confirms existing intuition — what the signal actually supports once you've honestly accounted for the noise.

I moved from experimental high-energy-density physics into professional baseball operations because the underlying methodology is identical: build rigorous probabilistic models, test them against expensive data, and make decisions under meaningful uncertainty.
