Operating Principles

Leadership Philosophy

These aren't aspirational statements — they're operating constraints. Violating them consistently produces bad science and bad organizations.

Principle 01

Servant Leadership

The job of leadership is to remove obstacles, provide clarity, and create conditions where talented people can do their best work. Authority is a tool for enabling others, not for self-amplification.

Good decisions come from the person closest to the problem, not necessarily the most senior person in the room.

Principle 02

Integrity as a Practical Necessity

Intellectual honesty isn't a virtue signal — it's a prerequisite for doing useful work. In science and in sports analytics, the cost of believing a false model is real and measurable.

Being honest about what the data says, including when it contradicts your prior or your preferred narrative, is the only sustainable path.

Principle 03

Psychological Safety & Failure

Discovery requires being wrong. If the environment punishes people for surfacing a bad result, reporting a failed experiment, or disagreeing with leadership, you've destroyed your own feedback loop.

Building high psychological safety isn't soft management — it's sound epistemology. The team that can fail openly is the team that learns fastest.

“The team that can fail openly is the team that learns fastest.”
